About Southern Districts Cricket Club, Darwin

Get to know The Crocs

Our Story

Southern Districts Cricket Club Inc. has been a proud part of the Northern Territory cricket community since its founding. Based at Gerry Wood Oval in the Freds Pass Reserve, the club competes in the Darwin & Districts Cricket Competition and offers inclusive programs for juniors, seniors, and women — fostering participation and growth at every level.

Established in 1981 with the formation of a D Grade team, the club became an incorporated association in 1982. A turf wicket was installed in 1985, paving the way for the club's entry into the A Grade competition in 1987.

Today, Southern Districts Cricket Club continues to promote teamwork, development, and community spirit through the game of cricket.

Club Mission

To provide a welcoming and competitive environment for cricketers of all ages and abilities in the rural area, fostering skill development, sportsmanship, and a lifelong love for the game, while actively contributing to our local community.

Our Facilities

Our club boasts excellent facilities to support our players and members.

  • Well-maintained turf pitches.
  • Modern training nets for practice sessions.
  • A comfortable clubhouse with amenities.
  • Ample parking and spectator areas.
  • Modern change rooms and shower facilities.
  • Equipment storage and maintenance areas.
  • Scoreboard and electronic timing systems.

How to Join

We welcome players of all ages and skill levels. Whether you're interested in senior cricket, women's cricket, or junior programs, there's a place for you at Southern Districts Cricket Club.
